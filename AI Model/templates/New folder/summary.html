Analysis Summary

Features Signification
Mutation Type The type of genetic alteration present in the sequence, such as deletions, insertions, or substitutions.
Ancestral origin Indicates the geographical origin of the genetic variation. This helps in tracing human ancestry and understanding genetic diversity.
Liklihood of Mutation A probability score (on a scale from 0 to 1) estimating how likely a mutation is to occur in this gene.
Genetic Condition The disease or disorder associated with the mutation. Ex- Huntington's Disease, a neurodegenerative disorder
Inheritance type Describes how the mutation is passed down from parents. It can be either Dominant or Recessive.
Pathogenic score A numerical score indicating how harmful or disease-causing the mutation is. A higher score suggests a greater risk.
GC Content The percentage of guanine (G) and cytosine (C) bases in the DNA sequence. A higher GC content generally makes DNA more stable and affects gene expression.
Evolutionary Clade Indicates the broader classification in evolutionary terms. Ex - "Reptile" suggests an ancestral link in evolutionary history.
Protein synthesis effect Specifies whether the mutation affects protein production.
